Alappuzha

Alappuzha (formely known as Alleppey) is the administrative headquarters of Alappuzha District of Indian State Kerala. Also known as Venice of the East, it is considered as the oldest planned city in the region and the lighthouse built on the coast of the city is the first of its kind in the Laccadive Sea coast. You can climb to the top of the lighthouse for a small fee (it will cost extra for photography). Having said this, this place is an important tourist destination in India.

Alappuzha beach is another important tourist destination and its near to the lighthouse. There is remains of a sea walk and it is an important indication that this is the Alappuzha Beach. Mulakkal Chirappu, held in the end of December every year and annual Nehru Trophy Boat Race held in Punnamada Lake attracts millions of international and domestic tourists every year. The backwaters of Alappuzha is one of tourists favorite spot and a houseboat cruise can be booked too. THis connects to Qilon to South and Kumarakom as well as Cochin to North.

Other attractions include Pathiramanal Island, Pazhaveed Bhagavathi Temple, St. Andrew's Bascilica, Arthunkal and so on. Malayalam is the local language and you can get some really good coir products like mats. Apart some this, Karimeen Vazhayilalil Pollichathu, Prawns, etc are sure to delight your taste buds. Grab those local cusines too!
